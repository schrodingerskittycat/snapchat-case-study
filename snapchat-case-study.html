Project summary

I designed and shipped interactive AR lenses in Lens Studio with the goal of improving engagement and completion for Gen Z users. The work combined trend research, quick prototyping, and iterative testing to create playful, shareable experiences that aligned with platform goals.

Problem

Certain lenses were seeing drop-offs in interaction and low completion rates. The challenge was to design lenses that felt instantly understandable, visually current, and shareable for Gen Z.

My role

AR/UX intern — responsible for research, prototyping in Lens Studio, visual design, and cross-team collaboration.

Lens Studio UX Thinking User Research Prototyping Visual Design

Research & insights

I analyzed top-performing lenses, tracked engagement drop-off points, and studied Gen Z visual trends and interaction patterns. Key insights: users prefer quick, clear interactions; bold, transformative visuals increase completion; audio-reactive and motion-triggered effects boost shareability.

Ideation & prototyping

I sketched multiple concepts (transformations, motion triggers, social hooks), built quick Lens Studio prototypes, and ran small informal tests to evaluate which interactions felt intuitive and rewarding.

Solution

The final lens combined trend-forward visuals, clear trigger points, and concise feedback loops so users always knew how to interact. Animations were tuned for instant satisfaction and social sharing.

Impact

Post-launch dashboards and internal feedback showed improved interaction depth and more frequent shares. The experience aligned with product goals and gave actionable learnings for future AR features.

Reflection

I learned how small visual decisions shape behaviour, how to balance creativity with product constraints, and how to communicate effectively with cross-functional teammates to ship polished AR experiences.
