Snapchat AR Design Case Study

By Syeda Zuhaira Afreen • AR/UX Intern • Nov 2023 – Apr 2024

Overview

As an AR/UX intern at Snapchat, I designed interactive AR experiences focused on engagement, shareability, and Gen Z behavior patterns. My responsibility was to research trends, build prototypes in Lens Studio, and ship lenses that improved completion rates and user interaction quality.

Problem

Existing lenses showed declining completion rates and drop-offs in interaction. Gen Z users expect instant clarity, bold visuals, and high shareability. The challenge was to design an AR experience that felt intuitive within seconds.

Research Insights

Methods used: Lens performance dashboards, trend analysis, Gen Z behavior research, quick user tests.

Key findings:

  • Bold, transformative visuals lead to higher completion rates.
  • Motion-triggered actions improve average interaction depth.
  • Gen Z responds strongly to shareable, playful effects.
  • Clarity in the first 2 seconds determines whether users stay.

Solution

I built AR prototypes with trends-informed visuals, clear interactions, and quick gratification loops. The final design included motion-triggered effects, audio-reactive elements, and strong visual identity aligned with Gen Z preferences.

Impact

Internal dashboards showed improvements in interaction depth and share frequency. The lens aligned well with platform objectives and user expectations, and the learnings were used to guide future AR interactions.
