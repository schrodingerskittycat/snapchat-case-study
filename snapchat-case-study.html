Snapchat AR Lens — Case Study

By Syeda Zuhaira Afreen • AR/UX Intern • Nov 2023 – Apr 2024

Overview

During my internship at Snapchat AR Design, I worked on building interactive AR experiences for Gen Z users on Lens Studio. My focus was on designing lenses that felt intuitive, visually current, and aligned with evolving platform behaviors.

Lens Studio Product Thinking User Research AR Prototyping Visual Design

Problem

Several existing lenses showed declining engagement and lower completion rates among Gen Z users. The challenge was to design AR experiences that were instantly understandable, trend-aligned, and shareable.

Approach

I conducted targeted research on Gen Z interaction behaviors—examining top lenses, drop-off patterns, and trending visual styles. I supplemented this with quick user feedback loops and data from performance dashboards.

Findings showed that Gen Z responded best to: • Quick interactions • Bold transformations • Audio-reactive effects • Motion-triggered animations

Execution

I ideated multiple lens concepts using sketches, motion tests, and in-app prototypes. I focused on:

  • Clear, instant interaction triggers
  • Trend-forward visual language
  • Fast reward cycles to boost engagement
  • Shareable effects optimized for selfie mode

After iteration cycles, I produced a polished final Lens Studio build and collaborated with engineers and product designers to refine animations and ensure platform consistency.

Outcome

The redesigned experience showed strong improvements across interaction depth, completion rates, and share frequency. Internal reviews highlighted its clarity, aesthetic relevance, and fit with Snapchat’s AR ecosystem.

Reflection

This project strengthened my understanding of how product thinking, research, and rapid prototyping shape AR experiences. I learned how small design details influence behavior and how cross-functional teams collaborate to deliver high-quality user experiences.
